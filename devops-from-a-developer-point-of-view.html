Is devops even being done at ecobee?
  • a very brief view of the devops model is generally considered to be operations and development working closely together throughout the product development process

    note: this isn't describing the role of a devops engineer, it's more about the devops process
  • my version, and how I really think about devops, is that it's not just operations and development working closely together, it's operations, development, quality assurance and security all working closely together or even better all on the same team
  • this can be very relatable to Agile; certainly one brand of agile says that ideally a team should have members on it for all tasks and skills required to successfully deliver a product

    note: there should still be other members and roles on a team of course, but these are the ones that broadly fit into the devops model
  • going by this, most of us here are doing some form devops now

    any team with QA & engineers together could already be considered as part of a devops model

Why should developers care?

Automation
Observability
Reliability
  • not an exhaustive list!
  • but these are the ones that resonate with me the most, where I've had the most pain points in the past
  • to some extent, all of these things are already done at ecobee, but are maybe taken for granted
  • but they are all things that quite often devs will think of as outside of their scope or control
  • automation

    what does it get: faster releases, faster dev feedback cycle, faster testing anecdote on manual process: back at in the olden days, deployments were frequently done manually... I've worked in environments where they were done by scp'ing files to a production server, or by logging into a production server and running `git pull` and then restarting a service

  • observability

    monitoring: once code is released, that's not the end step for a developer, being able to monitor the status of your deployed application is necessary

    debugging: ever try debugging something and wishing you had more access to logs that you don't directly control? for instance web server logs, database logs, file system logs

    the devops model, where ops is either integrated with your team or conversing on a daily basis helps with all these things

  • reliability

    anecdote on testing: for a while one company I worked with had a single QA team, they would write out test cases for each dev team, the backlog of them was long, and their backlog of executing the tests was even longer
